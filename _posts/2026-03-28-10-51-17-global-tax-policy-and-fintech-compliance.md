# Navigating the New Normal: Global Tax Policy, Crypto, and the Rise of AI Compliance

The global tax and financial landscape is undergoing a profound transformation, with 2025 and 2026 emerging as critical years for sweeping regulatory changes. From multilateral agreements reshaping corporate taxation to the intricate dance of virtual asset regulation, businesses face an unprecedented compliance challenge. Yet, amidst this complexity, a powerful counter-trend is gaining momentum: the explosive growth and strategic investment in AI-driven fintech solutions designed to navigate this new normal.

At the heart of the corporate tax revolution lies the OECD's Pillar Two initiative. Described by EY as creating a "new tax normal," these side-by-side rules demand significant readiness from multinational enterprises. Along with CRS 2.0 reshaping international information exchange, these frameworks signify a concerted global effort towards greater tax transparency and minimum taxation. For companies, understanding and implementing these new mandates, as highlighted by EY's essential actions for Pillar Two readiness, is no longer optional but imperative for avoiding controversy.

Parallel to this, the world grapples with the burgeoning realm of virtual assets. The period of 2025/26 is seeing intense focus on global crypto policy reviews, with jurisdictions like the UAE actively charting roadmaps for virtual-asset leadership. Navigating this evolving terrain requires a keen eye on developments like new IRS reporting rules, impacting not just traditional finance but also digital asset holders. The appointment of former OECD advisor Colby Mangels to Taxbit, a leading crypto tax solutions provider, underscores the deep intersection of policy expertise and fintech innovation in this space.

Indeed, the sheer complexity of these regulatory shifts is fueling massive investment in compliance technology. Andreessen Horowitz's lead in a $21M Series A for Sphere, an AI-native tax compliance software, along with Yonda Tax bagging $15M to expand its global compliance tech, signals a clear market demand. These investments reflect the urgent need for scalable, intelligent solutions capable of interpreting intricate rules, automating data flows, and ensuring accuracy across diverse jurisdictions. Technology is no longer just a support function; it's becoming the primary enabler for effective compliance.

The impact of these policy shifts is also felt at the jurisdictional level. While some nations like Puerto Rico have strategically leveraged policies like Act 60-2019 to transform their fintech landscape, others, such as Vietnam, find themselves on lists of non-cooperative jurisdictions, leading to significant compliance implications. This disparate approach highlights the importance for businesses to not only understand global mandates but also to meticulously track regional nuances and their potential impact on operations.

In essence, the coming years will be defined by an intricate interplay of policy, technology, and geopolitical dynamics. The convergence of global tax reforms, maturing crypto regulation, and the rise of sophisticated AI-native compliance solutions paints a picture of a challenging yet navigable future. Businesses that proactively embrace technological innovation and maintain a sharp focus on evolving policy will be best positioned to thrive in this rapidly transforming global economy.
